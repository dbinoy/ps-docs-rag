# Business Continuity Program – Architect Brief

Document Scope

This 1-page overview introduces Central 1's Business Continuity Program (BCP)—a formal framework encompassing policies, definitions, and governance structures for operational resilience across the organization.

Architecture-Relevant Elements

Key Components:

  • Established BCP policies and standards
  • Defined business continuity procedures and responsibilities
  • Recovery objectives and operational continuity strategies

Critical for Payment Solutions Design:

  • Payment systems must align with BCP recovery time objectives (RTOs) and recovery point objectives (RPOs)—likely critical infrastructure with minimal downtime tolerance
  • Design redundancy, failover mechanisms, and data replication into payment processing flows
  • Document dependencies: identify single points of failure in payment rails, settlement processes, and member-facing interfaces
  • Ensure backup and recovery capabilities for payment data stores and transaction logs

Integration Constraints:

  • Any payment system enhancement must include BCP compliance assessment
  • Disaster recovery testing protocols may impact production environments
  • Payment data must support rapid recovery; validate backup/restore procedures in architecture reviews

Action Items:

Review full BCP documentation to determine specific RTO/RPO targets for payment systems before finalizing architecture designs. Coordinate with BCP governance on recovery testing schedules.
