# Electronic Bill Payments System – Architecture Brief

Overview

Central 1's Electronic Bill Payments System enables financial institutions to submit customer-initiated bill payments through a shared infrastructure, with settlement and reconciliation managed centrally.

Key Data Flows & Integration Points

  • Submission: Two integration paths—file transmission or direct connection to eCommSwitch
  • Settlement: Posted to institution accounts by 5:30 am PT (8:30 am ET) next business day
  • Core Database: Maintains biller registry (with Biller Identification Numbers), customer biller accounts, and payment records

Critical Architecture Elements

  • Biller Onboarding: Institutions perform enhanced due diligence on restricted entities; BID assignment gates biller participation
  • Reconciliation Responsibility: Institutions must reconcile posted customer charges against Central 1 settlement totals
  • Reversals & Trace: System supports payment cancellations and trace requests for discrepancies (Section 12)

Design Constraints

  • Fixed settlement window (overnight batch)
  • Multi-institution shared system—isolation and audit trail critical
  • Reconciliation logic must handle timing mismatches between customer-facing and settlement systems
  • Report generation required for multiple institution types

Before Designing Enhancements

Clarify: real-time vs. batch settlement requirements, biller onboarding workflow automation scope, and reconciliation exception handling depth.
