# AgriInvest Program – Architecture Brief

Overview

AgriInvest is a government agricultural income-protection program administered by AAFC, with Central 1 Credit Union acting as the hub for member financial institution participation. Member credit unions open and manage AgriInvest accounts for qualified agricultural producers.

Key Architectural Data Flows

  • Account Lifecycle: Member institutions open accounts (subject to AAFC approval); account closure requires AAFC authorization
  • Activity Reporting: All transactions recorded in AgriInvest Activity Files, submitted semi-monthly to Central 1 (reporting periods: 1–15th and 16th–EOM; deadline: 3rd business day of following period)
  • Compliance Tracking: Member institutions choose recordkeeping approach (forms storage or alternative methods)

Critical Rules & Constraints

  • Member institutions must execute Participant's Agreement with Central 1 before enrollment
  • 100% of AgriInvest accounts must be reported; no exceptions
  • Semi-monthly cadence is non-negotiable (regulatory requirement)
  • AAFC holds approval authority for account openings/closings—member institutions act as service layer only

Design Implications

  • Account status transitions depend on external AAFC decisions (async workflows)
  • Activity file format and submission mechanism are critical integration points
  • Audit trail and recordkeeping flexibility requires configurable storage/metadata strategies
  • Plan for reconciliation between member institution records and Central 1 submissions
