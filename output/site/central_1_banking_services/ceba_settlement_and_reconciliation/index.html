# CEBA Settlement and Reconciliation – Architecture Brief

Overview

This document group covers the operational workflows for Canada Emergency Business Account (CEBA) loan repayments, administration fees, settlement, and reconciliation. Central 1 acts as intermediary between member financial institutions and Export Development Canada (EDC).

Key Data Flows

1. Loan Repayments: Member institutions report repayments to Central 1 via ServiceNow twice monthly; Central 1 aggregates and submits to EDC

2. Admin Fees: Member institutions report fees end-of-month; Central 1 processes for settlement

3. Settlement: Funds posted in lump sum to member accounts same business day; net reconciliation occurs post-submission

4. Returns: EDC funds returned for duplicates, cancellations, or other exceptions

Critical Business Rules

  • Bi-weekly repayment reporting cycle (EDC-mandated)
  • Monthly fee reporting deadline
  • Same-day settlement posting requirement
  • Central 1 acts as clearing house and settlement authority

Architecture Considerations

  • Integration points: ServiceNow portal, EDC submission interface, member institution accounts
  • Reconciliation logic: Must handle fund returns, duplicate detection, and net settlement calculations
  • Audit trail: Track reporting submissions, EDC acknowledgments, settlement transactions
  • Idempotency: Prevent duplicate posting in settlement process
