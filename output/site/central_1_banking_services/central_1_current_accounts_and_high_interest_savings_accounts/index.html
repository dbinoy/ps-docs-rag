# Brief Summary: Central 1 Current Accounts & HISA

Overview

This document set covers operational management of Central 1 deposit accounts used by BC/Ontario credit unions: Current Accounts (settlement vehicles) and High Interest Savings Accounts (investment products).

Key Architectural Elements

Current Accounts:

  • Settlement hub for all financial activity and service fees across Central 1 products
  • Funding sources: cheques, AFT file credits, internal transfers
  • Inter-branch and corporate client transfers via ServiceNow integration
  • Multi-branch reconciliation via OREC file (Account Reconciliation Service—subscription-based)

HISA:

  • Low-risk deposit investment option generating yield
  • Separate product line from operational current accounts

Critical Design Constraints

1. Data Flows: AFT files, OREC reconciliation files, ServiceNow transfers—batch and potentially real-time

2. Settlement Rules: All product activity and fees must flow through current accounts; tight coupling between products and settlement

3. Reconciliation: Multi-branch institutions require automated matching (OREC); assumes daily/periodic batch cycles

4. Integration Points: ServiceNow (transfers), legacy banking systems (cheques, AFT), Central 1 backend

5. Regulatory Context: BC/Ontario credit union requirements; multi-institution coordination

For enhancement design: Assume batch-first architecture with file-based interfaces; clarify OREC reconciliation SLAs and AFT processing windows.
