# Central 1 Banking Services – Section Overview

Purpose

This section documents Central 1's settlement, account management, and reporting infrastructure for credit unions participating in government-backed lending programs (AgriInvest, CEBA) and daily payment operations. It establishes the operational and technical requirements for maintaining compliant current accounts, executing bi-monthly reconciliations, and integrating with federal fund flows (EDC, AAFC).

Key Concepts

1. Current Accounts (Settlement Base): Every participating financial institution maintains a mandatory Canadian dollar current account at Central 1 serving as the settlement hub for all product activity, fees, and inter-institutional transfers. Balances refresh every 20–30 minutes via CAS Online.

2. Semi-Monthly Reporting Cycles: AgriInvest and CEBA both operate fixed reporting windows (1–15th, 16–end of month for AgriInvest; twice-monthly for CEBA repayments). Reports must be submitted within three business days of period close via ServiceNow.

3. Dual Authorization Path: Credit unions open accounts and manage participants, but Agriculture and Agri-Food Canada (AAFC) validates eligibility; similarly, EDC funds CEBA loans but Central 1 executes settlement.

4. Participant Agreements: Financial institutions must sign Central 1 Participant Agreements to participate; this is a legal gate for system onboarding and determines policy scope (recordkeeping, user access).

5. Activity Files & Reconciliation: All account activity (including zero-activity accounts) flows through standardized Activity Files; multi-branch institutions can subscribe to OREC (Operational Reconciliation) for automated matching against submitted data.

6. Fund Flow Timing: CEBA funds post as lump sums same-day-of-receipt; repayments must be reported twice monthly to meet EDC deadlines; settlement to current accounts is immediate.

How It Works

1. Financial institution signs Participant Agreement with Central 1 and maintains current account.

2. Institution opens participant accounts (AgriInvest or CEBA) via application; AAFC/EDC validates eligibility.

3. Account activity occurs; institution records all transactions on Activity Files.

4. During reporting period close (within three business days), institution submits Activity Files or repayment/fee forms via ServiceNow or CAS Online.

5. Central 1 validates submissions, posts funds/fees to current account, and generates settlement advices.

6. Institution reconciles via CAS Online (near real-time balance view) or OREC (automated multi-branch matching).

7. Reports become available on Client Centre for audit/compliance review.

Integration Points

  • ServiceNow: CEBA repayment and administration fee submissions; inter-branch fund transfers.
  • CAS Online: Real-time balance queries, 12-month transaction history, Account Groups for role-based view filtering.
  • Client Centre: Portal authentication hub; posting advices, statements, and downloadable reports.
  • EDC (Export Development Canada): External fund source; Central 1 is intermediary for CEBA settlement.
  • AAFC (Agriculture and Agri-Foods Canada): External eligibility authority for AgriInvest participants.

Architect Notes

  • No API Published: This section describes UI-driven and batch file processes only. No REST/SOAP APIs are documented; all integrations assume file-based or portal-mediated submission.
  • User Access Isolation: CAS Online user provisioning is manually managed by Central 1—not self-service via Admin or IMS. Enforce principle of least privilege via Account Groups.
  • Recordkeeping Discretion: Financial institutions may archive forms locally or rely on Central 1 retention; audit trail responsibility is ambiguous—clarify with legal before designing data deletion policies.
