# Canadian Dollar Money Orders – Architecture Brief

Overview

This document set covers the operational and compliance framework for Canadian dollar money orders issued directly by credit unions. Money orders function as cash equivalents and represent a direct liability against the issuing institution.

Key Architectural Considerations

Core Business Process:

  • Ordering → Stock Control → Sale (payment required upfront) → Voiding/Refunding → Daily Reconciliation → Clearing

Critical Data Flows:

  • Issuance tracking with unique identifiers
  • Real-time inventory management (stock control against physical shipments)
  • Transaction records for compliance (AML, identification requirements)
  • Daily reconciliation: issued vs. cleared money orders
  • Tracing capability for dispute resolution

Key Business Rules:

  • Cash-equivalent treatment (payment collected before issuance)
  • Void only on error or cancellation during transaction
  • Daily reconciliation mandatory
  • Full audit trail and record retention required per regulations

Integration Points:

  • General ledger (debit on issuance)
  • Clearing house systems
  • Client Centre digital portal
  • Inventory management systems

Design Constraints:

  • Regulatory compliance (identification, KYC, record-keeping)
  • Audit and traceability requirements (critical for reconciliation)
  • Cash-like liability model (immediate GL impact)
  • High assurance around voiding/refunding processes

For Enhancement Planning: Prioritize reconciliation automation, audit trail robustness, and clearing integration.
