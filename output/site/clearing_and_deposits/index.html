# Clearing and Deposits – Section Overview

Purpose

This section covers Central 1's end-to-end payment clearing and deposit processing workflows for member credit unions. It encompasses the receipt, validation, imaging, and settlement of deposit items (cheques, money orders, drafts, Receiver General Warrants) through multiple capture channels (Branch Capture, Corporate Capture, Mobile Deposit), as well as the handling of incoming clearing items and dishonoured cheques. This is a core operational domain managing daily fund flows and regulatory compliance.

Key Concepts

1. Clearing Process: Exchange and reconciliation of payment items between financial institutions, resulting in funds transfer. Central 1 acts as the clearing intermediary for credit union members.

2. Deposit Channels: Three remote deposit mechanisms—Branch Capture (institution-side scanning), Corporate Capture (business customer self-service), and Mobile Deposit (customer mobile app)—plus traditional paper deposits. Physical items are not retained; imaging is authoritative.

3. Cheque Imaging & Online Return System (ORS): Central 1 captures and stores images of deposit items and incoming clearing items. Credit unions access via ORS portal for retrieval, chargeback handling, and reconciliation rather than requesting physical items.

4. Money Orders: Credit union–drawn instruments requiring dual controls: order-to-stock management (printed on demand by Westkey/D+H), daily reconciliation of issued vs. cleared amounts, and void/refund procedures with audit trails.

5. Settlement & Timing: Deposits credited same-day upon receipt/processing at Central 1. Canadian and US dollar deposits processed through distinct workflows. MERG file and reports confirm receipt and reconciliation status.

6. Legal & Compliance: Institutions must enforce CPA Rules (Payments Canada), FINTRAC reporting, AML/KYC identification requirements, record-keeping mandates, and cheque validity standards (CPA Standard 006 for MICR encoding).

How It Works

1. Deposit Capture: Items scanned via Branch Capture, Corporate Capture, or Mobile Deposit; images transmitted to Central 1.

2. Validation & Acceptance: Central 1 validates MICR, item format, and acceptability; rejects invalid items (returned to originating institution).

3. Imaging & Indexing: Cheque images stored and linked to member accounts; available in ORS for 180 days.

4. Settlement: Credits posted to credit union's Central 1 account same-day; reconciliation via MERG files and deposit reports.

5. Returns & Chargebacks: Dishonoured items identified by drawee institution, returned through ORS or manual entry; notice of dishonour issued per CPA rules.

6. Reconciliation: Daily reconciliation of money orders and deposits; aged deposits (>24 hours open state) escalated for management.

Integration Points

  • Client Centre: Portal for user access, Branch Capture/Corporate Capture application gateway.
  • ORS (Online Return System): Image retrieval, return processing, inquiry resolution.
  • MERG File: Batch settlement and reconciliation data delivery.
  • Payments Canada (CPA): Rule compliance; clearing and return rules.
  • Member Core Systems: Deposit posting, statement generation with images.
  • Paystation Portal: Scanner procurement for Branch/Corporate Capture.

Architect Notes

  • No Physical Retention: Images are definitive; institutions cannot request physical cheques except for unqualified items and chargebacks. Impacts audit trail and dispute resolution design.
  • 180-Day Image Window: ORS data availability expires after 6 months; long-term archival strategy required.
  • Aged Deposit Escalation: 24-hour transmission SLA for Branch Capture deposits; violations trigger escalation workflows and reporting overhead.
  • User Role Complexity: Four Branch Capture user types (Capture, Reviewer, Supervisor, Admin) with nested approval workflows
