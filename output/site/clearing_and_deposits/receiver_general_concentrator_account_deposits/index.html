# Receiver General Concentrator Account Deposits – Architecture Brief

Purpose

This document group defines Central 1's procedures for managing federal department and agency deposits to the Receiver General of Canada through concentrator holding accounts. It covers the complete lifecycle: account opening, deposit preparation, processing, error/chargeback management, and settlement.

Key Data Flows

1. Account Setup: Central 1 → preferred FI (on behalf of federal entity)

2. Deposit Submission: Federal departments/agencies → concentrator account

3. Processing: FI processes deposits; Central 1 manages PAD creation and settlement

4. Error Handling: Dishonoured PADs, deposit errors, chargebacks routed through defined procedures

5. Settlement: Central 1 credits the FI; cheques processed via branch capture

Critical Architecture Considerations

  • Transit identifiers: Branch number (5-digit) + institution number required for routing
  • PAD management: Central 1 creates and manages pre-authorized debits; dishonour scenarios must be handled
  • Multi-party reconciliation: Federal agencies, FIs, and Central 1 require coordinated settlement
  • Error states: Chargeback and deposit error handling procedures must integrate with core processing
  • Regulatory compliance: Receiver General settlement requires audit trails and precise account mapping

Integration Points

Transit number composition, PAD processing systems, branch capture, settlement engines.
