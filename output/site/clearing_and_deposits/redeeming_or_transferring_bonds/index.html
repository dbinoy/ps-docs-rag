# Briefing Summary: Bond Redemption & Transfer Operations

Overview

This document group defines Central 1's operational role as agent for credit unions in redeeming and transferring government and savings bonds. It covers certificate-style bonds, coupon bonds, marketable instruments (Government of Canada Bonds, Treasury Bills), and provincial instruments (Ontario Savings Bonds).

Key Operational Flows

Redemption pathways:

  • Certificate-style Canada Savings Bonds (R/C series)
  • Legacy coupon/fully-registered bonds (Series 1-31, all matured)
  • Marketable bonds at maturity
  • Individual coupons post-maturity date
  • Ontario Savings Bonds (provincial)

Transfer pathway:

  • Government of Canada bonds via Central 1 submission

Unmatured sales:

  • Market-rate liquidation through Central 1

Critical Architecture Requirements

1. Identity verification required for Registered Owners (Authorized Official documentation needed); account at credit union not required

2. Bond type classification drives distinct redemption rates and processing rules

3. Maturity date logic gates coupon and bond redemption eligibility

4. Physical custody flow: Credit unions → Central 1 collection → settlement

5. Rate determination varies by bond series and instrument type

Design Considerations

  • Support multiple bond taxonomies and redemption policies
  • Implement maturity date validation gates
  • Handle authorized signatory verification workflows
  • Integrate with settlement/clearance systems
