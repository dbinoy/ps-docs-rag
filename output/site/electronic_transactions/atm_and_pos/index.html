ATM and POS Document Group Summary

Scope: Central 1's settlement framework for ATM and POS transactions across THE EXCHANGE® and ACCULINK® networks, covering acquirer/issuer procedures, adjustments, traces, and settlement.

Key Architectural Elements:

  • Dual Network Architecture: THE EXCHANGE® and ACCULINK® (debit-only) networks serve different transaction types and participants
  • Role-Based Processing: Acquirer (ATM/POS owner) and Issuer (card issuer) have distinct responsibilities and data flows
  • Settlement Model: Daily net settlement posts to credit unions' Central 1 current accounts; USD converted to CAD
  • Adjustment/Trace Workflow: Network-dependent (direct peer-to-peer vs. Central 1-mediated); routed by transaction type and requester role
  • Regulatory Requirements: PCMLTFA compliance, card capture protocols, audit trails for all adjustments

Critical Design Constraints:

  • Transactions must route correctly based on network and role
  • Settlement must be atomic and reconcilable
  • Trace requests require bidirectional communication with external networks
  • Card capture is mandatory; destruction must be documented
  • Multi-currency handling (USD→CAD conversion) at settlement

Integration Points: C2C Node, Cooperative Node, external switching suppliers, and interbank networks.
