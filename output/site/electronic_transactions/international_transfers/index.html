# International Transfers Architecture Brief

Overview

International Transfers is a Central 1 payment product enabling customers to send funds globally via online banking and mobile app, powered through an Agility partnership. Settlement and operational handling align with existing electronic bill payment processes.

Key Architectural Components

Data Flow & Integration:

  • Customer-initiated transfers via digital channels → Agility partner integration
  • Settlement process mirrors EBP (electronic bill payment) settlement framework
  • Reports and reconciliation use existing EBP infrastructure

Operational Processes:

  • Traces & Reversals: Managed identically to EBP traces; use BPCV (Bill Payment Confirmation View) and BPRE (Bill Payment Reversal/Error) reports for case management
  • Settlement & Reporting: Leverages established EBP settlement and reconciliation mechanisms

Critical Design Constraints

1. Dependency on EBP Architecture: Any enhancement must maintain compatibility with bill payment settlement and reporting systems

2. Partner Integration: Agility handles international execution; Central 1 manages domestic rails

3. Operational Parity: Trace/reversal workflows should replicate EBP patterns to minimize training and process divergence

4. Reporting: Must output through existing BPCV/BPRE reporting structures

Architect Considerations

  • Clarify settlement timing and SLA differences vs. domestic transfers
  • Confirm Agility API contracts and failure scenarios
  • Define fund flow timing to/from partner
  • Map reconciliation gaps at partner handoff points
