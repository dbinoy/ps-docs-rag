# FX Drafts Enrollment Summary

- **Purpose**: Documents the enrollment process and setup configuration for financial institutions participating in Central 1's FX Drafts product, managed in partnership with Convera.

- **Enrollment workflow**: Institutions must execute agreements with Convera and Central 1; Central 1 assigns access to User Management Security Officers, who then provision other Client Centre users; requires completion of Form 6299 (FX Drafts Customization).

- **Key configuration dimensions** captured in Form 6299: settlement options, reporting options, authorization levels, and dollar amount thresholds—these specifications are institution-specific and drive Convera's implementation approach.

- **Multi-party governance**: Central 1 owns enrollment package distribution and access provisioning; Convera owns product demonstrations, training, demo environment provisioning, and implementation of customization settings.

- **Critical integration points**: 
  - Form 6299 submission via ServiceNow or email (support@central1.com) triggers Central 1 → Convera handoff for implementation
  - Demo environment must reflect institution-specific Form 6299 specifications before go-live
  - Client Centre serves as the portal for form access and user access assignment

- **Constraint to note**: Pre-implementation training and demo environment are conditional deliverables dependent on completed enrollment agreements and Form 6299 submission.

- **Change management**: Form 6299 serves as the post-enrollment change notification mechanism for customization updates after go-live.
