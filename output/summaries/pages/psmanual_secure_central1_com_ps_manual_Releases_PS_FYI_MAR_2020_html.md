# FYI – March 2020 Summary

- **Purpose**: Documentation of regulatory authority transitions in BC and Ontario credit union sectors, with corresponding updates to Payment Solutions Manual references and contact information.

- **BC Regulatory Change**: FICOM operations ceased November 1, 2019; BCFSA became the new regulator for BC credit unions and administrator of Credit Union Deposit Insurance Corporation. All manual references updated accordingly.

- **Ontario Regulatory Change**: FSRA assumed regulatory functions from FSCO and DICO effective June 8, 2019. All manual references to both legacy entities updated to FSRA.

- **Documentation Updates Scope**: Changes applied across two volumes with specific section impacts:
  - **AFT Volume**: Sections 1.4 and 2.1 (Contacts/Definitions) in both CAFT and File Transmission parts
  - **Electronic Transactions Volume**: Sections 1.5, 2.1, and Chapter 3 (Contacts/Definitions/Legal Considerations) in Funds Transfers part

- **Integration Point Dependencies**: Any downstream systems or processes relying on regulator contact information, regulatory references, or compliance citations require updates to reflect BCFSA/FSRA; legacy FICOM and FSCO/DICO integrations must be deprecated.

- **Architectural Constraint**: Legal and compliance sections contain regulatory citations that directly impact transaction processing rules and validation logic; architects must verify all legal/regulatory dependencies are remapped to current authorities before system changes.
